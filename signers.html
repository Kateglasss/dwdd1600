Who Were the Signers

Famous Signers

Some of the most famous signers of the Declaration of Independence include:

John Hancock (Massachusetts)
President of the Continental Congress, remembered for his famously bold and oversized signature.
Thomas Jefferson (Virginia)
The principal author of the Declaration and later the third U.S. President.
Benjamin Franklin (Pennsylvania)
A leading statesman, diplomat, inventor, and one of the most influential Founding Fathers.
John Adams (Massachusetts)
A major advocate for independence and later the second U.S. President.
Samuel Adams (Massachusetts)
A leader in organizing resistance to British rule, often called the "Father of the American Revolution."
Roger Sherman (Connecticut)
The only person to sign all four great founding documents of the United States.
Charles Carroll (Maryland)
The only Catholic signer of the Declaration, also the last surviving signer.
Richard Henry Lee (Virginia)
Introduced the resolution calling for independence from Britain in June 1776.

Each of these individuals played a significant role in the founding of the United States and is remembered for their courage in signing the Declaration, an act considered treasonous by the British Crown.
